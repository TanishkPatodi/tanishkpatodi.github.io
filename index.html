About Me

I am broadly interested in understanding phenotypic heterogeneity in biological systems. In practice, this interest currently takes the form of modeling metabolism to explore how Mycobacterium tuberculosis develops drug tolerance. I completed my Integrated Masters in Biotechnology from D.Y. Patil University (Pune).

Recently, I have grown to really appreciate networks and graph theory, and I am excited to explore how these ideas, together with machine learning approaches, can help make sense of structure, robustness, and heterogeneity in biological systems.

Outside of research, I enjoy bike rides, exploring mountains, and learning about different cultures, especially through local food. And of course, I'm always up for music recommendations, both lyrically or composition wise great music.

Research Interests

  • Genome-scale metabolic modeling
  • Drug tolerance in tuberculosis
  • Phenotypic heterogeneity and metabolic adaptation under stress conditions
  • Biological networks and graph theory
  • Transcriptomics
