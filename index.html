About Me

I am a PhD student in the IMI Department at IISc. I completed my Integrated Masters in Biotechnology and am currently working on modeling metabolism to understand drug resistance in Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

Recently, I have developed a strong appreciation for the structure and behavior of networks, particularly through the lens of graph theory, and I am keen to explore how these ideas illuminate robustness and heterogeneity in biological systems.

Outside of research, I enjoy long bike rides, exploring mountains, trekking, and discovering music—especially music with strong lyrical depth.

Research Interests

  • Genome-scale metabolic modeling
  • Drug resistance and persistence in tuberculosis
  • Redox heterogeneity and metabolic adaptation
  • Biological networks and graph theory
  • Systems biology of host–pathogen interactions
