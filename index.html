Tanishk Patodi

PhD Student, IMI Department
Indian Institute of Science (IISc)

I am broadly interested in understanding phenotypic heterogeneity in biological systems. My current work focuses on modeling metabolism to study drug tolerance in Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

I am particularly drawn to networks and graph theory, and I am exploring how these ideas, together with machine learning approaches, can help make sense of structure, robustness, and heterogeneity in biological systems.

Outside of research, I enjoy bike rides, exploring mountains, and learning about different cultures — especially through local food. I am also always happy to discover new music, whether it stands out for its lyrics or for the way it is composed.
